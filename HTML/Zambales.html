Zambales, Central Luzon

Province of
Zambales

Stretching along the West Philippine Sea, Zambales is a province of coves, islands, and mango orchards. Its coastlines, upland forests, and farming communities create a landscape where marine livelihood and mountain agriculture thrive together. From San Antonio to Iba and Subic, each town adds to a culture shaped by resilience, trade, and deep ties to land and sea.

02
Agriculture

Fertile
Harvests

Zambales relies on coastal fisheries, mango farms, coconut-growing communities, and upland crop production. Fisherfolk and farmers work side by side to sustain local markets, food processing, and household livelihoods. Seasonal harvests, small-scale enterprises, and agri-tourism continue to strengthen the province's food economy across municipalities.

Nature & Heritage

Coves, islands, and forested peaks create a landscape where heritage, nature, and outdoor travel meet. Trails, surf breaks, river routes, and community festivals make Zambales both a cultural destination and an ecological gateway in Central Luzon.

The Flavors of
Zambales

Zambales cuisine centers on fresh seafood, orchard produce, and comforting home-style recipes passed down in coastal communities. Grilled, sour, and coconut-rich dishes reflect everyday life by the sea, while mango-inspired desserts showcase one of the province's most celebrated harvests.

    Experience the Vibrant Festivals of Zambales

    Festivals of Zambales

    Zambales festivals celebrate harvest cycles, indigenous identity, and community pride. From the Dinamulag celebrations of mango abundance to coastal events and cultural gatherings honoring Aeta heritage, each festival brings together local craft, food, music, and intergenerational traditions that keep provincial identity alive.

    Scroll to discover
    ZAMBALESFEST
    Top 1
    01

    Dinamulag Festival (Iba)

    A province-wide celebration of Zambales mangoes featuring trade fairs, farm showcases, culinary events, and community street performances.

    The festival highlights local growers, agri-entrepreneurs, and youth groups while promoting value-added products and farm-tourism opportunities.

    Top 2
    02

    Coastal Adventure Week (Subic)

    A week of water sports, beach programs, and outdoor exhibitions that highlight Zambales' coastline and marine tourism corridors.

    Visitors enjoy guided coastal tours, seafood bazaars, local enterprise booths, and cultural showcases that support fisherfolk and small businesses.

    Top 3
    03

    Aeta Heritage Day (Botolan)

    A cultural gathering that honors indigenous Aeta traditions through music, dance, oral storytelling, and craft demonstrations.

    Community exhibits spotlight ancestral knowledge, woven products, local produce, and intergenerational learning on heritage conservation.
