Official Overview

Central Luzon: The Rice Granary of the Philippines

Region III is a vast fertile plain located north of Manila. It serves as the primary source of the country's rice supply, making it a critical pillar of national food security. Beyond its fields, it is a booming hub for Agri-Business and Digital Transformation.

01 / 07

Aurora

Gateway to the Pacific Coast

Facing the vast Pacific Ocean, Aurora is a sanctuary where the rugged Sierra Madre mountains meet turquoise waters. Known as the "Birthplace of Philippine Surfing" in Baler, it also holds deep historical roots as the home of President Manuel L. Quezon and the site of the historic Siege of Baler.

Explore Coastline
02 / 07

Bataan

Land of Valor & Progress

A peninsula steeped in heroism, Bataan is home to the Shrine of Valor (Mount Samat) which honors the bravery of soldiers during WWII. Today, it has transformed into a major industrial powerhouse with its Freeport Area, while maintaining eco-tourism gems like the Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar.

View Heritage
03 / 07

Bulacan

The Cradle of Noble Heroes

The historical heart of the Philippines, Bulacan is the site of the Malolos Congress and the iconic Barasoain Church. It is a province celebrated for its exquisite craftsmanship in jewelry, pyrotechnics, and leather, as well as its rich culinary traditions and vibrant festivals like the Singkaban.

See Traditions
04 / 07

Nueva Ecija

The Rice Granary of the Philippines

Serving as the backbone of national food security, Nueva Ecija is the leading producer of agricultural staples. Beyond its endless golden rice fields, it is a hub for agricultural research and home to the stunning Minalungao National Park, offering a rising destination for agri-tourism.

Agricultural Tour
05 / 07

Pampanga

Culinary Capital of the Philippines

A world-class destination for gastronomy, Pampanga offers a sensory feast of Kapampangan flavors. It is also known as the "Christmas Capital," famous for its Giant Lanterns, and serves as a global gateway through the bustling Clark Freeport Zone and International Airport.

Taste the Culture
06 / 07

Tarlac

The Melting Pot of Central Luzon

Tarlac is a unique blend of cultures, serving as the meeting point for Ilocano, Pangasinan, Pampango, and Tagalog influences. From the spiritual Monasterio de Tarlac to the rugged terrain of Mount Pinatubo, it offers a diverse landscape of history and eco-adventure.

Explore Crossroads
07 / 07

Zambales

The Chrome & Surf Destination

Renowned for its crystalline coves like Anawangin and the world-famous surfing swells of San Felipe. It is a key player in the maritime industry through Subic Bay and a top producer of the sweetest mangoes in the world, blending industrial growth with tropical paradise.
